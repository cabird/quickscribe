# Exploring a Move to Google's AI Platforms Team
- **Duration:** 31m
- **Attendees:** Chris, Speaker 2
- **Purpose:** Discuss Chris's interest in exploring a move to Speaker 2's Google AI/platforms organization, assess fit, and surface next steps.

## Executive Summary
Chris is exploring options outside his current team and is considering a potential move to Speaker 2's AI/platforms organization at Google. Speaker 2 described the org structure, product areas (platforms, Dev AI, UX/UXR, instrumentation, data science) and the kinds of problems the team is solving (product feature velocity, token usage telemetry, prompt-level instrumentation, and tooling to measure AI impact). Chris and Speaker 2 agreed to stay in touch: Chris will meet Eduardo next week and will send a written summary of his interests; Speaker 2 will watch for relevant role openings as org changes settle. Open items include specific role level, compensation, reporting line, and exact data/privacy access for prompt-level telemetry.

### Discussion Threads

#### Chris's motivation and career priorities
Context: Chris explained why he's exploring options and what he values in a team.
- Chris said he enjoys empirical studies followed by building productized solutions, and currently feels his work is "tolerated" rather than embraced on his present team.
- Chris values building things people use over publishing papers, wants meaningful product impact, and needs roles compatible with family/time constraints.
- Chris confirmed he is open to leaving the known for a role that better fits his research-to-build DNA.
Outcome: Open — Chris will explore opportunities; confirmation of next step is his meeting with Eduardo and sending a write-up.

#### Compensation and level expectations
Context: Chris raised compensation and level as practical considerations.
- Chris shared current compensation context (base ~270k, total ~600k, at level 67).
- Speaker 2 described typical salary/level ranges at several companies (base ranges from ~250–400k; total comp neighborhoods 800–1.5M possible depending on role and company) and commented that level assignments at Google can map differently to external bands.
Outcome: Open — exact level and comp for any role remain to be determined during recruiting conversations.

#### Team/org description and primary work areas
Context: Speaker 2 described current org structure, recent reorganizations, and core workstreams.
- Speaker 2 explained the split: developer/dev-AI work, Core Data, AI Foundations (platforms), and the recent moves that folded many groups into AI-focused platforms. UX/UXR is being added, expanding qualitative and mixed-methods capacity.
- Key work includes: product feature velocity (PFV) metrics; token usage vs outcomes; instrumentation and data warehousing; prompt- and token-level telemetry; building dashboards, nudges, and interventions; and partnering with finance and privacy for cost/usage controls.
- Speaker 2 noted the team needs both strategy and hands-on delivery and is hiring a technical researcher role that could be senior and report to them or a peer lead.
Outcome: Open — role exists conceptually; exact job posting/reporting line depends on an ongoing reorg and hiring cycle.

#### Instrumentation, data access, and privacy constraints
Context: Chris and Speaker 2 discussed the importance of prompt-level telemetry and privacy approvals.
- Speaker 2 emphasized the team's need for prompt-level data to correlate prompts, token burn, model choice, and outcomes, and is actively pushing privacy/leadership channels to get access.
- Speaker 2 described recent successful escalations and said they can provide air cover to unblock high-priority data requests, but privacy approval timelines can vary.
- Chris stressed that without prompt-level visibility you cannot correlate token usage with developer workflows.
Outcome: Open — team is pursuing access; privacy approvals and exact data availability remain unresolved.

#### Research cadence, deliverables, and speed of work
Context: Expectations about research timelines and nature of deliverables were discussed.
- Chris prefers short, impact-focused research that quickly informs product decisions and leads to built features rather than long academic-driven efforts.
- Speaker 2 prefers faster, iterative research that yields weekly/biweekly telemetry or bullet-point updates and quick pilots; criticized long, theoretical proposals that delay actionable insight.
- Both agreed the role should deliver rapid, tangible outcomes and partner closely with data science, UXR, and platform teams.
Outcome: Resolved — both prefer fast, product-focused research cycles; alignment on expectation to deliver quick, actionable insights and partner across disciplines.

#### Cultural fit and team dynamics
Context: Speaker 2 described cultural pockets and what makes a good fit.
- Speaker 2 emphasized value in people who ramp quickly, handle change, juggle multiple priorities, and move from insight to build. She noted some team members react badly to frequent priority changes.
- Speaker 2 suggested Chris's strengths (research+engineering, rapid ramp, flexible) map well to the team's needs and cautioned about certain reporting dynamics (e.g., Chris should not report to a peerlike manager such as Sierra in a way that creates awkwardness).
Outcome: Open — cultural fit is promising; final reporting structure and team placement are pending.
