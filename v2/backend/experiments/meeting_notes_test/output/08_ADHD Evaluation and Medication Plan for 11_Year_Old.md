# ADHD Evaluation and Medication Plan for 11‑Year‑Old
- **Duration:** 37m
- **Attendees:** Casey, Chris, Erin, Dr. Peltier
- **Purpose:** Evaluate ADHD symptoms for Casey and create an initial medication and follow-up plan

## Executive Summary
The team reviewed Casey's ADHD symptoms, mood, and current counseling; all agreed that medication may speed symptom control while therapy continues. Dr. Peltier recommended starting an extended‑release stimulant (Adderall XR) at 10 mg daily as a two‑week trial, with parents to report effects via MyChart and an in‑person follow‑up in one month for weight and blood pressure. The family and clinician confirmed contingency plans for switching to short‑acting formulation if needed and for requesting brand‑name medication if supply issues arise. Remaining open items include final response to the starting dose, potential need for an additional evening dose, and any supply/insurance issues.

### Discussion Threads

#### ADHD diagnosis, strengths, and goals
Context: Review of recent forms and clinical presentation to confirm ADHD and set treatment goals.
- Dr. Peltier noted the intake forms were consistent with ADHD and reviewed that ADHD often includes strengths (creativity, divergent thinking) as well as challenges with school structure and emotional regulation.
- Casey described mood as “a mix of feelings” and that worrying has improved; Casey reported difficulty controlling anger at times.
- Erin confirmed Casey is already in weekly CBT-style therapy.
- Outcome: **Resolved** — ADHD diagnosis supported by forms and history; treatment goals are to improve attention, school functioning, and emotional/impulse control (Dr. Peltier confirmed).

#### Medication options: extended‑release (XR) vs short‑acting stimulant
Context: Decide formulation, dosing, and practicality for school day.
- Dr. Peltier recommended starting with a low dose of long‑acting stimulant (Adderall XR 10 mg) because it may cover the school day with one dose and reduce the need for school‑day dosing logistics.
- Chris and Erin noted prior family experience: Parker and Riley use short‑acting Adderall twice daily; parents are comfortable navigating twice‑daily dosing if necessary.
- Erin asked about capsule beads and administration; Dr. Peltier clarified Adderall XR may be opened and the beads mixed with food (applesauce) if needed for swallowing.
- Outcome: **Resolved** — Trial plan: start Adderall XR 10 mg daily for two weeks (agreed by family and Dr. Peltier). If insufficient effect or tolerability issues, consider short‑acting formulation or dose adjustment.

#### Side effects and monitoring plan
Context: Discuss common stimulant side effects and safety monitoring.
- Dr. Peltier reviewed expected side effects to watch for: decreased appetite, sleep disturbance, increased blood pressure/heart rate; other possible effects include dry mouth, stomach ache, and tremor.
- Dr. Peltier recommended baseline and follow‑up vitals and weight; plan to check weight and blood pressure at one month.
- Outcome: **Resolved** — Parents will monitor side effects; in‑person follow‑up scheduled in one month for vitals and dose adjustment as needed.

#### Supply, insurance, and brand vs generic concerns
Context: Discuss potential pharmacy shortages and insurance coverage for brand name medication.
- Chris explained prior experiences with generic shortages and willingness to pay out of pocket for brand name if needed; asked whether prescriber can specify brand name to get insurance coverage.
- Dr. Peltier agreed to prescribe brand name upon request and advised sending a MyChart message if shortages occur.
- Outcome: **Open** — Strategy agreed (request brand if shortage), but actual supply/insurance outcome remains dependent on pharmacy and insurer.

#### Care continuity and psychiatry collaboration
Context: Clarify who will manage medication and whether next week's psych appointment is needed.
- Erin asked whether to keep the upcoming psych appointment; Dr. Peltier said the medication can be managed by their team and the psych visit could be canceled.
- Family preferred keeping treatment consolidated with known providers; they confirmed weekly therapy will continue.
- Outcome: **Resolved** — The family may cancel next week's psych visit; Dr. Peltier’s team will manage the initial medication trial. (Erin to cancel the psych appointment.)

## Decisions
1. Dr. Peltier will prescribe Adderall XR 10 mg once daily as a two‑week trial for Casey (confirmed by Dr. Peltier and parents).
2. The family will monitor effects and side effects and send a MyChart message after the two‑week trial to report response (confirmed by Erin and Chris).
3. An in‑person follow‑up visit with vitals and weight will occur in one month to assess effect and safety (confirmed by Dr. Peltier and parents).
4. The family will cancel the unrelated psychiatry appointment next week (confirmed by Erin).
